# **Fire & Rescue Signals**

<Aside type="caution" title="Medical / Fire Response">

- **Code Blue**: Lost pulse *(Start CPR)*
- **Code Red**: Activate trauma code *(5‑minute timer)*
- **Priority 1**: Highest priority transport
- **Priority 2**: Medium priority transport
- **Priority 3**: Low priority transport *(Routine)*

</Aside>

## **EMS Triage – START**

<Aside type="tip" title="Simple Triage and Rapid Treatment">

Use **RPM** checks:

- **R – Respirations**: Not breathing → open airway; if still apneic → **Black**. >30/min → **Red**.  
- **P – Perfusion**: Cap refill >2s **or** no radial pulse → **Red**.  
- **M – Mental**: Cannot follow commands → **Red**.  
- Otherwise → **Yellow** (delayed). Walking wounded → **Green**.

Tag, count, and radio a **MCI report** with totals by color.

</Aside>

## **Primary Trauma Survey – MARCH**

<Aside type="note" title="Tactical / Trauma Order">

**M**assive hemorrhage → tourniquets, wound packing.  
**A**irway → NPA/OPA as appropriate.  
**R**espiration → seal chest wounds, manage ventilations.  
**C**irculation → treat shock, IV/IO per protocol.  
**H**ypothermia/Head injury → keep warm, monitor neuro.

</Aside>

## **CPR / Cardiac Arrest Quick Sheet**

<Aside type="danger" title="Adult BLS Snapshot">

- Confirm **unresponsive & apneic**; call **Code Blue**.  
- Start **compressions** (≥100–120/min, depth ~2 in, full recoil).  
- **AED** ASAP; follow prompts.  
- **Ratio** 30:2 without advanced airway; continuous compressions with 10 breaths/min if airway placed.  
- Rotate compressors every 2 minutes; minimize pauses.

</Aside>

## **Patient Report Formats**

<Aside type="note" title="Give Good Radio / Handoff">

- **MIST**: *Mechanism/Medical complaint, Injuries/Illness, Signs & vitals, Treatments*.  
- **SAMPLE**: *Symptoms, Allergies, Meds, Past hx, Last intake, Events*.

</Aside>

## **Normal Adult Vitals (Field Quick View)**

<Aside type="tip" title="Recognize Sick vs Not Sick">

- **HR**: 60–100 bpm  
- **RR**: 12–20 /min  
- **SBP**: ≥90 mmHg (concern if &lt;90)  
- **SpO₂**: ≥94% room air (titrate O₂ as needed)  
- **GCS**: 15 normal; &lt;13 concerning

</Aside>

## **Fireground Basics**

<Aside type="caution" title="First‑Due Size‑Up (CAN)">

- **C**onditions: Building type, smoke/fire showing, exposures.  
- **A**ctions: What your unit is doing (investigating, fire attack, search).  
- **N**eeds: Water supply, additional alarms, utilities, law/EMS.

</Aside>

<Aside type="note" title="Water Supply & Staging">

- Establish / confirm **hydrant** and lay‑in if needed.  
- Declare **Level 1 Staging** for incoming units; keep street clear of the scene.  
- Assign **IC** (Incident Command) and **TAC** channel early.

</Aside>

<Aside type="danger" title="MAYDAY Criteria (Any = Call It)">

- Lost, trapped, entangled, low air, fall through/near collapse, separated from crew.  
- State **MAYDAY**, unit, location, problem, air %, actions. Activate **RIT**.

</Aside>

## **Hazmat Awareness (Ops‑Level Reminder)**

<Aside type="caution" title="Defensive Posture">

- Uphill, upwind, upstream.  
- **Isolate & deny entry**, establish hot/warm/cold zones.  
- Use **ERG** guides; call **Hazmat Team**; do not rush in.

</Aside>

## **Transport Priorities**

<Aside type="tip" title="Go or Stay?">

- **Load‑and‑go**: uncontrolled bleed, airway compromise, tension pneumo, GCS &lt;13, penetrating torso trauma, amputation, burns with airway risk.  
- **Stay‑and‑play**: time‑critical interventions required before movement (e.g., airway, hemorrhage control).

</Aside>
